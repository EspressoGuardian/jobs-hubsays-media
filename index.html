Low-Risk Launch Rules

  • No user accounts, no resume uploads, no saved-job backend, and no email capture in v1.
  • Show metadata and short expat snapshots only, not copied full job descriptions.
  • Public apply actions should go straight to the employer or official source.
  • Visa and language signals are guidance only, never guarantees.
  • Correction and removal handling should stay visible from day one.

Public Launch Recommendation

  • Public beta is live on GitHub Pages while the custom-domain path is finalized.
  • Target custom domain remains jobs.hubsays.media once the Cloudflare zone and token path are aligned.
  • Keep hubsays.com personal and non-product-facing.
  • Move to a standalone jobs brand later only if the product earns a clean spin-out.
  • Monetize with affiliate offers first, then consider ads later.

Why This Niche

  • English-speaking jobs in the Netherlands and EU have clear expat demand.
  • Visa-sponsored and HSM-likely roles solve a sharper problem than generic “jobs in Europe” pages.
  • Language-specific pages create stronger intent than generic “jobs in Europe” content.
  • Map plus filters is more useful than another giant listicle.

Trust And Legality

  • Phase 1 uses jobs only, not republished company news.
  • Housing in this preview is city-level signal data only, not scraped listing inventory.
  • Public ingest should start from official career pages, approved APIs, and permitted feeds.
  • Do not scrape LinkedIn or copy full job descriptions at scale.
  • Preview mode stays tracker-free; any future ads or tracking stay behind consent.
  • Do not process recruiter names, resumes, or candidate profiles into your own database.

Partner Tools And Revenue Posture

Affiliate-first and sponsorship-later. No display ads in this preview.

Monetization order

Affiliate links and free templates first. Keep AdSense off by default so the product stays fast, tracker-light, and less exposed to programmatic-SEO quality hits.

Analytics

PostHog remains off in local preview. If enabled later, start with event-only analytics and no replay.

Cookie banner

A simple first-party banner is enough for a tracker-light MVP. EEA AdSense requires the certified consent path.

Live + work positioning

Differentiate from Google Maps by scoring relocation decisions, not by trying to index every restaurant, shop, or place pin.

Individual liability posture

Lowest-risk public launch is an informational jobs navigator: no user accounts, no CV intake, direct employer apply links, and business-specific insurance confirmed in writing.
