Data Sources

Hubsays is designed as a source-first navigation layer. Public pages should explain the source classes behind the product, the licence posture, and the fact that users should verify role details at the original source.

What should be public-facing

  • High-level source classes such as official employer pages, structured ATS endpoints, official public statistics, and factual infrastructure datasets.
  • Representative source links so users can see the kind of public data being used.
  • Licence posture: public-domain first where possible, attribution-required sources called out clearly where used.
  • Source attribution, freshness, and direct-link routing on the actual product surface where available.

Representative public-signal sources suitable for enrichment or reference:

  • Eurostat data for wages, labour-market context, and comparable EU statistics.
  • OECD Better Life Index for quality-of-life and well-being context.
  • EURES for official EU job-mobility context and public employment signals.
  • Make it in Germany: Opportunity Card for current Germany job-search visa guidance.

Strict no-obligation lane

If “no obligations required” is interpreted strictly, the usable all-EU source set is much smaller than people expect. Current clean candidates for this lane are:

  • Wikidata for CC0 entity facts such as city, airport, and transport-hub metadata.
  • OurAirports for public-domain airport metadata and downloadable airport tables.

Those sources are suitable for zero-signup infrastructure signals such as nearest airport, airport code, or major transport-node context.

Free but attribution-required lane

Most of the genuinely valuable EU-wide data lives here instead. It is still free and usable, but not obligation-free. Examples include weather, transport, and official statistical layers.

  • Eurostat for labour, wage, cost, and unemployment context.
  • European Central Bank data portal for macro and currency context.
  • European Environment Agency data hub for air-quality, climate, and noise layers.
  • data.europa.eu as a master portal for many EU public datasets, each with its own licence.
  • Open-Meteo for weather and forecast signals if attribution fits the product posture.
  • OpenStreetMap / Overpass for transport-hub and walkability context, with attribution obligations.

Not in scope:

  • LinkedIn scraping
  • Large-scale copied job-description text
  • Recruiter names, direct emails, or other personal data stored as product content
  • Republished full news articles or large snippets
In this beta, product data is refreshed in bounded batches with source attribution, freshness fields, and direct links back to the original source rather than copied full listings.
