Data Sources

Current beta posture: static JSON files maintained in-repo, short source-first summaries, and direct-link routing rather than copied full job descriptions.

Current structured layers in this beta:

  • data/jobs.json: seeded role metadata for public-beta testing of filters, compare logic, and relocation scoring.
  • data/cities.json: city-level relocation signals such as rent pressure, English friendliness, and move-score inputs.
  • data/countries.json: country-level context used by the index and compare views.

Approved source posture for expansion:

  • Official company career pages
  • Structured ATS endpoints such as Greenhouse, Lever, Workable, Recruitee, and Teamtailor where allowed
  • Approved partner APIs and feeds reviewed against contract, attribution, and privacy risk
  • Dutch sponsor lists and public-registry enrichment where they add a real factual signal
  • Official or public statistical layers for relocation context, not fake precision

Examples of the next feed classes under review, not all live yet:

  • Public ATS exports with clean metadata contracts
  • Approved remote-job APIs such as Jobicy where the feed can be normalized honestly
  • Public city/country datasets that improve rent-pressure and relocation context

Official public-signal sources already suitable for enrichment or reference:

  • Eurostat data for wages, labour-market context, and comparable EU statistics.
  • OECD Better Life Index for quality-of-life and well-being context.
  • EURES for official EU job-mobility context and public employment signals.
  • Make it in Germany: Opportunity Card for current Germany job-search visa guidance.

Strict no-obligation lane

If “no obligations required” is interpreted strictly, the usable all-EU source set is much smaller than people expect. Current clean candidates for this lane are:

  • Wikidata for CC0 entity facts such as city, airport, and transport-hub metadata.
  • OurAirports for public-domain airport metadata and downloadable airport tables.

Those sources are suitable for zero-signup infrastructure signals such as nearest airport, airport code, or major transport-node context.

Free but attribution-required lane

Most of the genuinely valuable EU-wide data lives here instead. It is still free and usable, but not obligation-free. Examples:

  • Eurostat for labour, wage, cost, and unemployment context.
  • European Central Bank data portal for macro and currency context.
  • European Environment Agency data hub for air-quality, climate, and noise layers.
  • data.europa.eu as a master portal for many EU public datasets, each with its own licence.
  • Open-Meteo for weather and forecast signals if attribution fits the product posture.
  • OpenStreetMap / Overpass for transport-hub and walkability context, with attribution obligations.

Privacy-safe analytics and ad surfaces

  • Cloudflare Web Analytics: lowest-ops aggregate measurement lane.
  • Plausible: privacy-first aggregate analytics with no behavioural profile model.
  • Fathom: another cookieless measurement surface.
  • Umami: self-hostable analytics if the lane should stay local-first.
  • EthicalAds and Carbon Ads: better later, after the site has repeat traffic and a stable layout.

Not in scope:

  • LinkedIn scraping
  • Large-scale copied job-description text
  • Recruiter names, direct emails, or other personal data stored as product content
  • Republished full news articles or large snippets
Update cadence today is governed and manual. The long-term posture is deterministic refresh of static JSON with source attribution, freshness fields, and direct links back to the original source.
