Methodology

The Hubsays Live + Work Index is a decision-support layer for internationals. It does not rank candidates, score resumes, or automate job decisions. It compares cities and countries on work, housing, cost, and language signals.

Current scoring pillars

  • Jobs: target-skill role density and visible role volume in the current beta dataset.
  • Housing: rent level and rent-to-income pressure.
  • Cost: a city or country cost-of-living index relative to an EU baseline.
  • Language: English-first fit in jobs plus practical daily-life accessibility.

Current move-score contract

In the current public beta, the city-level move score is a compact relocation signal built from the scored city contract already visible in cities.json. The public contract currently exposes component scores for jobs, housing, cost, and language. Visa posture is shown separately as a filter and narrative signal rather than as a hidden black-box multiplier.

This is intentional: the beta should help a person decide where a move is realistic, not pretend to be an all-knowing recruiter score.

Data model

The beta currently uses a stable contract:

  • cities.json: identity, raw metrics, and derived scores per city
  • countries.json: macro comparison layer per country
  • jobs.json: seeded role examples for the beta interface

Source posture

The public beta currently uses seeded data. The intended long-term source posture is governed official sources: employer career pages, allowed ATS endpoints, and selected public statistics. The product should not scrape LinkedIn or store copied full job descriptions at scale.

What the beta does not claim yet

  • It does not yet publish ghost-job judgments or company hiring-pattern claims.
  • It does not yet show salary-after-tax or disposable-income estimates as if they are precise facts.
  • It does not yet infer relocation difficulty from private user data or stored profiles.
Current version: 2026-v1. Changes to the meaning of scores should stay explicit and versioned.
