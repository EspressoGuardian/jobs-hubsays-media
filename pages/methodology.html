Methodology

The Hubsays Live + Work Index is a decision-support layer for internationals. It does not rank candidates, score resumes, or automate job decisions. It compares cities and countries on work, housing, cost, and language signals.

Current scoring pillars

  • Jobs: target-skill role density and visible role volume in the preview dataset.
  • Housing: rent level and rent-to-income pressure.
  • Cost: a city or country cost-of-living index relative to an EU baseline.
  • Language: English-first fit in jobs plus practical daily-life accessibility.

Data model

The preview now locks a stable contract:

  • cities.json: identity, raw metrics, and derived scores per city
  • countries.json: macro comparison layer per country
  • jobs.json: seeded role examples for the preview interface

Source posture

Public launch should move from seeded preview data to governed official sources: employer career pages, allowed ATS endpoints, approved partner feeds, and selected public statistics. The product should not scrape LinkedIn or store copied full job descriptions at scale.

Current version: 2026-v1. Future releases should keep the contract stable and log changes in a simple changelog rather than silently shifting the meaning of scores.
